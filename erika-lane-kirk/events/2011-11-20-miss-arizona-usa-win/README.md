## Summary

Erika Frantzve won the Miss Arizona USA 2012 pageant on November 20, 2011—her 23rd birthday. This was her second attempt at the title, having placed first runner-up in the 2011 competition. She went on to represent Arizona at Miss USA 2012 but did not place.

---

## Context

Erika Frantzve first competed in the Miss Arizona USA pageant system at the 2011 competition, where she earned first runner-up and "caught the eye of both officials and fans." She returned the following year and won the crown on her birthday.

The Miss Arizona USA pageant is part of the Miss USA system, which at the time was sponsored by Donald Trump. Winners go on to compete at the national Miss USA pageant.

## Subject's Statements

### On Being a "Tomboy"

> "I didn't wear my first pair of heels until I was 14 years old, but I had a really mean lay-up."

### On Why She Entered Pageants

> "My mom used to take us to soup kitchens and constantly said we needed to share our blessings with others. When I learned that by competing I could touch more people, further my causes, I knew it was a chance to make a greater impact."

### On the Meaning of the Crown

> "Don't tell anyone, but that sparkly tiara is only a little plastic hat. What really matters is making a difference, and I want to change the world. The bling is just a bonus."

### Athletic Background

Before pageants, Frantzve played basketball for two years on the women's team at Regis University in Denver, Colorado. She described basketball as her "first love" and took a break from pageants to focus on college athletics.

### Nonprofit Work

Frantzve founded "Everyday Heroes Like You" in 2006 to raise funds for under-recognized charities:

> "I have a passion to help people in general so when I was creating my nonprofit, I didn't want to focus on [just] kids or animals, I wanted to make it all-encompassing, find a way help everyone in a unified way."
